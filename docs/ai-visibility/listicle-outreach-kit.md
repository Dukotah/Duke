# Copper Bay Tech — "Get Listed on Best-Of Listicles & Directories" Outreach Kit

## Why this matters (the one-line strategy)

When someone asks an AI assistant "who's the best web designer in Sonoma County?", the model doesn't invent an answer — it leans on third-party listicles, directories, and roundup posts. Across studies of AI search citations, **listicles and directories account for roughly 17–21% of the sources LLMs cite** when answering "best X near me" questions. That's a bigger slice than most businesses' own websites get. Getting Copper Bay Tech onto those pages is one of the highest-leverage things we can do to show up in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews — not just in the ten blue links.

Two buckets do the work:
1. **Authority directories** (Clutch, GoodFirms, etc.) — structured, trusted, frequently scraped by AI for "top agency" lists.
2. **Editorial roundups** ("12 Best Web Designers in Santa Rosa") — written by humans, ranked by Google, and quoted nearly verbatim by AI.

Go after both.

## Part 1 — Prioritized Target List

Listed roughly in order of return-on-effort for a Santa Rosa / North Bay web + software shop.

### Tier 1 — Do these first (highest AI-citation value)

**1. Clutch.co**
- *What it is:* The most-cited B2B services directory; verified client reviews, ranked lists by city/service ("Top Web Developers in California / San Francisco Bay Area").
- *Why it matters for AI:* Clutch profiles and "Top…" pages are among the most frequently cited sources in AI answers about agencies. High domain trust.
- *Effort:* Medium — build a full profile, then collect 1–3 verified client reviews (they phone/email-verify). Reviews are the real unlock.
- *Cost:* Free to list; paid "sponsored" placement optional (skip at first).

**2. The Manifest**
- *What it is:* Clutch's sister site; publishes plain-language "best company" lists and business how-to content that ranks and gets quoted.
- *Why it matters for AI:* Editorial list format is exactly what LLMs love to extract. Inherits Clutch's review data.
- *Effort:* Low once Clutch is done — largely auto-populated from your Clutch profile.
- *Cost:* Free.

**3. Expertise.com**
- *What it is:* Editorial "best of" awards by city + category ("Best Web Designers in Santa Rosa"). They independently scout and select.
- *Why it matters for AI:* The page titles match user queries word-for-word; very strong "Best [service] in [city]" SEO. Frequently surfaced in AI local recommendations.
- *Effort:* Low — submit your business for consideration via their "get listed" form; selection is editorial.
- *Cost:* Free to be listed/awarded; optional paid badge/marketing kit.

**4. UpCity**
- *What it is:* Marketplace + directory of B2B service providers with city/service pages and "Top Provider" recognition.
- *Why it matters for AI:* Strong local SEO, review-driven, regularly cited in "top agencies near me" answers.
- *Effort:* Low–medium — claim/create profile, gather reviews.
- *Cost:* Free tier exists; paid Premium for lead access (optional).

### Tier 2 — High value, do next

**5. GoodFirms**
- *What it is:* Global research/review directory with ranked category + location lists (web dev, software dev, IT services).
- *Why it matters for AI:* Another structured, trusted source; you can list across multiple service categories (web, custom software, IT, cybersecurity) — multiplies surface area.
- *Effort:* Medium — profile + reviews; they verify.
- *Cost:* Free to list; paid for top placement (skip initially).

**6. DesignRush**
- *What it is:* Curated agency directory + frequent listicle publisher ("Top Web Design Companies in California / San Francisco").
- *Why it matters for AI:* Pumps out exactly the "Top…" articles AI quotes; decent authority.
- *Effort:* Low–medium — submit agency for the directory and for inclusion in roundups.
- *Cost:* Free listing; paid featured placement (optional).

**7. Bark.com**
- *What it is:* Service-matching marketplace; consumer-facing profiles for web design, IT support, etc.
- *Why it matters for AI:* Generates local profile pages and reviews; useful for "near me" coverage and a small lead trickle.
- *Effort:* Low.
- *Cost:* Free profile; pay-per-lead if you want introductions (optional).

### Tier 3 — Local trust + the long tail (don't skip — these are AI's "local" signal)

**8. Local roundup blogs / listicle posts**
- *What it is:* Independent articles like "Best Web Designers in Sonoma County," "Top Santa Rosa Web Design Companies," often on agency blogs, marketing sites, or local-business publications.
- *Why it matters for AI:* These city-specific human-written posts are disproportionately cited for local recommendations — and most are reachable by a simple email (see Part 2).
- *Effort:* Medium — find them, pitch the author one by one.
- *Cost:* Free (occasionally a small "sponsored listing" fee — judge case by case).

**9. Chamber of Commerce directories (Santa Rosa Metro Chamber, Sonoma County, Petaluma, Sonoma Valley, Healdsburg, Windsor, Sebastopol)**
- *What it is:* Member business directories for each North Bay chamber.
- *Why it matters for AI:* High local trust + ".org/business-directory" pages that reinforce "this company is real and in Sonoma County." Strong NAP (name/address/phone) consistency signal.
- *Effort:* Low (per chamber).
- *Cost:* Membership fee (usually modest, annual). Pick 1–2 most-relevant chambers to start (Santa Rosa Metro + Sonoma County EDB-adjacent).

**10. Foundational local citations (do in parallel — table stakes)**
- *What:* Google Business Profile (most important of all), Bing Places, Yelp, Apple Business Connect, Nextdoor, BBB, Houzz (if doing any design-adjacent work), Manta, LocalIQ/Yellow Pages.
- *Why it matters for AI:* Consistent NAP across these is the bedrock signal that AI and Google use to trust a local business exists and is what it says it is.
- *Effort:* Low each, tedious in bulk.
- *Cost:* Mostly free.

> **Sequencing tip:** Lock Google Business Profile first → Clutch (with reviews) → Expertise.com + UpCity → GoodFirms/DesignRush → then chip away at local roundups and chambers. Reviews are the recurring bottleneck, so start asking happy clients now.

> **Outreach notes:** Find the author's name (byline or About page), not "Hi there." Send from your real domain email. Mention exactly one work sample with a concrete result. Follow up once after 5–7 business days, then let it go. If a "listing fee" comes back, evaluate the site's actual traffic/authority before paying — many local roundups will add you for free.
