# Copper Bay Tech — First-Reviews Kit

A plug-and-play playbook to get a brand-new business its first batch of Google reviews — built so you can hand it to a non-technical owner and have them running by this afternoon.

---

## Why reviews are the #1 lever

For a new local business, Google reviews are the single highest-return thing you can work on:

- **They're the deciding factor.** When someone searches "[your service] near me," the businesses with reviews get the clicks. A listing with zero reviews looks risky; one with a handful of recent, specific reviews looks like the obvious choice.
- **They feed the map.** Google's local ranking leans heavily on review count, freshness, and the words inside them. Reviews that name your **service** and **city** literally help you show up for "[service] in [city]."
- **The first 5–10 do the heavy lifting.** Going from 0 to 5 reviews moves the needle far more than going from 50 to 55. This is the highest-leverage moment you'll ever have — don't waste it.

The goal isn't a perfect score. It's a steady trickle of **recent, specific, honest** reviews.

---

## The one rule: don't pay for reviews

Google's policy prohibits **incentivizing** reviews — no discounts, gift cards, raffle entries, freebies, or "leave us a review and get 10% off." It applies whether the incentive is for a positive review *or just any review*. Violations can get your reviews stripped or your listing suspended.

**What's allowed:** simply *asking* happy customers, making it easy with a direct link, and reminding them. That's the whole game. Ask everyone, the same way, every time.

---

## The 3-touch ask sequence

People mean to leave a review and then life happens. Three light, spaced touches — never nagging — is the sweet spot.

| Touch | When | How | Why it works |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. In person** | At the moment of delight — job done, invoice settled, customer visibly happy | Verbal ask + "I'll text you the link" | Catches them at peak goodwill |
| **2. SMS** | Within 1–2 hours, same day | Text with the direct review link | Phone is in hand; one tap to the form |
| **3. Email or SMS reminder** | 3 days later, only if no review yet | Short, friendly nudge | Covers the "meant to, forgot" crowd |

Stop after touch 3. If they haven't reviewed after a polite reminder, leave it — pushing further costs you goodwill.

**Setup before you start:** grab your direct review link from your Google Business Profile (Profile → "Ask for reviews" → copy link, or use your `g.page/r/...` short link). Paste that exact link into every template below where it says `[review link]`.

## SMS templates

Keep them short, human, and from a real person. Coach the customer to mention **what you did** and **the city** — that's what makes the review work for search. (Tip: fill in the brackets with *their* actual service so the hint feels natural.)

**SMS 1 — same-day ask**
> Hi [Name], it's Duke at Copper Bay Tech — great working with you today! If you've got 30 seconds, a quick Google review really helps a new local business like ours. A line on the [service] we did and that you're in [City] is perfect: [review link] Thank you!

**SMS 2 — softer / service-only**
> Hi [Name]! Glad we could help with your [service]. Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? Mentioning [City] helps neighbors find us. Takes a minute: [review link] — really appreciate it.

**SMS 3 — day-3 reminder (only if no review yet)**
> Hi [Name], just a friendly nudge — if you were happy with the [service], a short Google review would mean a lot to us as we get started: [review link] No worries if you're swamped. Thanks again!

> "If you're happy with how this turned out, the biggest favor you could do a new local shop is a quick Google review — I'll text you the link right now so it's one tap. Mention the [service] and that you're in [City] if you can!"

## Responding to reviews

Reply to **every** review, ideally within a business day. It signals you're attentive, and your replies are public — future customers read them.

**5-star reply**
> Thank you so much, [Name] — it was a pleasure helping with your [service]! We're proud to serve [City] and we're glad you're happy. Reach out anytime. — Duke, Copper Bay Tech

**Critical review reply** (stay calm, own it, take it offline)
> Thank you for the honest feedback, [Name] — I'm sorry this fell short of what you expected. That's not the standard we hold ourselves to. I'd like to make it right; please reach me directly at [email/phone] so we can sort it out. — Duke, Copper Bay Tech

Never argue, never get defensive, never share private details about the job in a public reply.

---

## Target cadence

The whole point is a steady, recent flow — Google rewards freshness, so don't blitz all your reviews in one day and then go quiet.

- **Week 1:** First **5 reviews.** Ask every single customer you serve, every time.
- **Weeks 2–4:** **2–3 per week** — get to ~10–12 total in the first month.
- **Ongoing:** Bake the ask into your closeout routine so **every happy customer** gets asked. Aim for **2–4 fresh reviews a month**, forever.

Track it simply: a spreadsheet with customer name, date asked (touch 1/2/3), and review yes/no. That's all you need to keep the trickle going.
