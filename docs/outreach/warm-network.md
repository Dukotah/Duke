# Warm-Network Blitz — send these today

The single fastest path to client #1. People who already trust you convert ~10×
a cold lead, and this needs **zero setup** (no domain verification, no tools).

## How to run it (20–30 min)
1. **Make the list (10 min).** Open your phone contacts + LinkedIn. Write down
   **30–50** people in these buckets:
   - Former coworkers / bosses / clients
   - Friends & family who **own** or **work at** a business
   - People in your network who *know* lots of local business owners (realtors,
     loan officers, contractors, bartenders, gym owners — connectors)
   - Anyone local you're on good terms with
2. **Send (15 min).** Text or email — one at a time, personalize the first line.
   It's a **referral ask, not a sales pitch.** Pick the closest template below,
   drop in their name, send.
3. **Track replies** in the CRM or a simple list. Any "yes, talk to ___" → call
   them within 24 hours while it's warm.

> Tip: lead with the **founding-client** angle — it gives people a concrete
> reason to refer you *now*, and a reason for the referred business to say yes.

---

## Templates (edit the first line so it's clearly personal)

### A. Friend / family / general
> Hey [name]! Quick one — I just launched Copper Bay Tech: I build websites and
> handle IT/security for small businesses here in Sonoma County. I'm taking on a
> few **founding clients at a big discount** to build up my portfolio. Know
> anyone whose website is a mess, who has no site at all, or who's stressed about
> getting hacked? I'll give them a free audit, no strings. Even just a name would
> mean a lot 🙏

### B. Former coworker / professional contact
> Hi [name] — hope you're doing well! I've gone out on my own: Copper Bay Tech,
> doing web design + IT/cybersecurity for Sonoma County small businesses. I'm
> looking for my first few clients and offering a founding-client rate. If anyone
> at [their company] — or anyone you know locally — needs a website built or
> fixed, I'd be grateful for an intro. Happy to return the favor any time.

### C. A connector (knows lots of owners)
> Hey [name] — you know everybody in [town], so I had to ask 😄 I just started
> Copper Bay Tech (websites + IT for local businesses) and I'm taking on a few
> founding clients at a discount. Who do you know whose website is embarrassing
> them, or who doesn't have one at all? I'll take great care of anyone you send
> my way — and there's a referral thank-you in it for you.

### D. Someone who owns a business themselves
> Hi [name]! I just launched my web + IT shop, Copper Bay Tech, for Sonoma County
> businesses. Totally not trying to sell you — but I'd love to do a **free audit**
> of [their business]'s site and tech setup and send you what I find. And if you
> know any other owners who could use a better website, I'm taking founding
> clients at a discount right now.

### E. Light follow-up (no reply after ~4 days)
> Hey [name] — no worries if you're swamped! Just bumping this in case it got
> buried. Even one name of a local business that needs a website would be a huge
> help as I get going. Thanks either way 🙏

## What "good" looks like
- 30–50 sent in week 1.
- A handful of intros — call each within a day.
- 1–2 of those become your **founding clients** (discount → review + case study +
  referral). That's the whole game; the rest compounds from there.
